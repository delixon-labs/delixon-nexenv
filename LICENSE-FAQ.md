# Delixon — License FAQ

Delixon is **source-available** software under the
[Functional Source License (FSL-1.1-ALv2)](LICENSE).

This is NOT open source in the traditional sense. The code is visible and
usable with one restriction: you cannot build a competing commercial product
with it. After 2 years, it converts to Apache 2.0 automatically.

## What you CAN do

- Use it internally for your own projects
- Study the code and learn from it
- Modify it for your personal or internal use
- Use it for non-commercial education and research
- Provide professional services to others who use it
- Contribute back to the project

## What you CANNOT do

- Sell or offer a commercial product or service that competes with Delixon
- Offer the same or substantially similar functionality as a commercial service
- Repackage it as your own product

## Common questions

**Can I use it at my company internally?**
Yes. Internal use is explicitly permitted.

**Can a consultant customize it for a client?**
Yes, as long as the client uses it internally and the consultant is not
selling a competing product.

**Does a private fork for internal use count as competing?**
No. Internal use is permitted regardless of company size.

**Can I build a SaaS that does the same thing?**
No. That would be a Competing Use.

**When does it become Apache 2.0?**
Two years after each version's release date. After that, you can do
anything Apache 2.0 allows — including commercial use.
